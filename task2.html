About Adam Sandler

Adam Sandler (born 1966) is an American actor, comedian, writer, and producer. Known for his comedic roles in films such as Happy Gilmore, Big Daddy, and Grown Ups, Sandler has also starred in critically acclaimed dramas like Uncut Gems. Over his career, he has become one of the most recognizable and influential comedy actors in Hollywood.

Life & Career Highlights

  1. Born in Brooklyn, New York (1966).
  2. Joined Saturday Night Live cast (1990–1995).
  3. Starred in hit comedies like Happy Gilmore (1996).
  4. Founded Happy Madison Productions (1999).
  5. Received critical praise for Uncut Gems (2019).

Notable Contributions

  • Revitalized slapstick and absurdist comedy in Hollywood.
  • Created his own production company producing dozens of films.
  • Donated millions to charities and humanitarian causes.
